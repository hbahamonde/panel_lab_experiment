You will now complete a short practice round.

This practice round is only meant to help you learn the interface. It is not part of the main study.

You have already answered some questions about your own policy priorities. In the main study, those earlier answers will later be connected to your final political decision.

On the next page, you will see 3 pieces of campaign news. You may choose which ones to open before making a political decision. Opening a new news item will reduce your available points, just as in the main study.

After reviewing the news, you will be asked to vote for a hypothetical political candidate. The purpose of this practice round is to help you become familiar with how the study works: how to open news items, how points decrease when you read more information, and how you will eventually make a vote decision.

In the main study, your final decision matters because it will be compared to the preferences you expressed earlier in the study. In other words, the study is interested in whether, given your own stated priorities and the information available to you, you choose the candidate who best matches those priorities.

You do not need to read every news item. Instead, choose the information that you think is most important for making an informed political decision.
