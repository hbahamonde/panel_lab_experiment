In this study, you will move through a hypothetical political campaign that unfolds over multiple waves.

Please imagine that you are living in a democratic society at a moment of rising political tension. Citizens are increasingly worried about the country’s direction, and public debate has become centered on whether existing institutions are strong enough to manage serious national challenges. Some people believe that the situation requires patience and institutional restraint, while others believe that the country may need faster and more forceful political action.

The campaign you are about to follow takes place in that setting. Over time, you will receive new pieces of information about the political environment, the candidates, and the broader social context. As in real campaigns, you will have to decide what information to pay attention to and how to use it when making political judgments.

In this first wave, you will answer a set of baseline questions about your policy preferences and your views about institutions and democratic risk. These answers will help establish your starting point before the campaign develops further.
