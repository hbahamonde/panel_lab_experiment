Study title: Political judgment and decision-making in a dynamic campaign environment

Researcher: Hector Bahamonde, Project Researcher

Contact: hector.bahamonde@utu.fi

Key information

You are being asked to participate in a research study. Participation is voluntary. Before you decide, it is important that you understand why the research is being done, what you will be asked to do, the possible risks and benefits, and how your information will be protected.

This study examines how people evaluate political information over time, how they respond to changing social and political conditions, and how they make political decisions in a hypothetical society. The study takes place over 3 waves:

Purpose of the research

You are being asked to participate in a research study about political judgment and decision-making. From this study, the researcher hopes to learn how people process political information across multiple stages of a campaign, how they update their views when conditions change, and how they make a final political choice.

What you will be asked to do

If you agree to participate, you will complete 3 waves of study activities over time. In each wave, you will review campaign-related information in a hypothetical society, open news items if you choose to do so, answer a small number of questions, and continue through the study tasks. In the final wave, you will cast a vote between two candidates in an election equivalent to choosing the prime minister.

Some study tasks require you to decide whether to open additional news items. Opening new items has a cost in points, and you will see your remaining point total on the screen. The same information budget carries across all 3 waves.

Potential benefits

You will not directly benefit from your participation in this study. However, your participation may contribute to a better academic understanding of how people process political information, update beliefs, and make political decisions in changing environments.

Potential risks

The foreseeable risks of participating in this study are minimal. Some questions or political scenarios may cause mild fatigue. Because the study asks about political judgments and decisions, some participants may find parts of the study sensitive or uncomfortable. You may skip a question only when the study design allows it, and you may stop participating at any time by closing the study.

Privacy and confidentiality

Your responses will be kept confidential to the maximum extent allowable by law. No unnecessary personal identifying information will be collected through the study interface. Study data will be stored securely and used only for research purposes.

The researcher will have access to the study data. Authorized university or research oversight personnel may also review study records if required for research oversight, ethics review, or legal compliance.

The results of this study may be published or presented at academic conferences or in other scientific outputs, but individual participants will not be identified in those reports.

Your rights to participate, say no, or withdraw

Your participation is voluntary. You have the right to say no. You may refuse to participate, and you may stop participating at any time by closing the study. There is no penalty for refusing to participate or for withdrawing.

However, full compensation depends on completing all 3 waves within the study schedule. If you withdraw early or do not complete all waves, you may not receive the full amount of compensation associated with the full study.

Costs and compensation for being in the study

There are no direct costs to you for participating in this study other than your time.

Your compensation includes a participation payment and a performance-based component. The performance-based component is part of the research design and is used to encourage careful attention and sincere decision-making during the tasks. In simple terms, the study rewards decisions that are consistent with your own preferences given the information available in the study. Compensation is not a study benefit; it is payment for participation and task performance.
