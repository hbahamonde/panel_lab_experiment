Each wave remains open for {{ wave_window_days }} days. Please use the same personal study link each time you return to the study. Unfortunately, if you miss one wave, you will not be able to participate in the upcoming waves.

Across these 3 waves, you will follow a political campaign in a hypothetical society. As the campaign unfolds, you will receive new information about social and political conditions, review campaign news items, and answer a small number of questions. In the final wave, you will cast a vote between two candidates in an election that is equivalent to choosing the prime minister. It is important that you participate in all 3 waves. Full compensation depends on completing the full study within the study schedule.

Each wave lasts about 20 minutes. In every wave, you will see a new set of campaign news items. These items are meant to resemble the kind of political information people encounter when deciding whether to click on digital news stories or political content on social media. The news changes across waves as the campaign progresses, so the information available to you later in the study will not be identical to the information available at the beginning.

Opening a new news item has a cost in points. Each point corresponds to a certain amount of money. As you open news items, your remaining points will decrease, and the updated amount will always be shown on the screen. For example, if you start with 50 points and each news item costs 5 points, opening 2 items will leave you with 40 points.

You keep the same information budget across all 3 waves. This means that points spent in one wave are no longer available in later waves. It also means that you must decide how much information to open at each stage of the campaign. Information you choose to learn earlier may help you make a more informed political decision later.

Your compensation includes a participation payment and a performance-based component. The performance-based component is part of the research design and is used to encourage careful attention and sincere decision-making during the tasks. In simple terms, the study rewards decisions that are consistent with your own preferences. For example, if a participant values one set of policies more than another, and the available information indicates that one candidate is a better match for those priorities, then choosing that candidate would count as the more accurate decision for that participant. In that sense, points are awarded not for choosing a particular candidate as such, but for making a decision that best matches your own stated preferences given the information available in the study.

At the end of the study, you will cast your final vote between the two candidates. Please read each page carefully and answer as thoughtfully as possible.
